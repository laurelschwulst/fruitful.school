How to Learn Online

We saw this photo on Instagram the other day and thought, ‚ÄúWow, this is a perfect encapsulation of what learning in the fall of 2020 can easily look like.‚Äù With many schools and institutions going completely virtual due to the global pandemic, there is a lot of pressure and expectation to continue the school year as normal.

This comic is quite refreshing and clear to us: Yes, learning remotely is HARD. But we‚Äôd argue it‚Äôs not impossible if you take a few things into consideration:

Learning online is completely different

Learning online is completely different than in person. This might seem obvious, but having students glued to a screen 100% of class-time does not make sense. Not all the learning has to happen from class-time, there will be very little required screen time and the screen time that we do have will mostly be to engage with each other.

Not all learning has to come from the teacher

We view teaching as creating and maintaining a healthy learning environment, so not all learning should come from directly the teacher. Learning happens many ways. Yes, the teachers will share a wealth of relative knowledge that will be curated, assigned, or suggested throughout the course. But you also have a community of students going through the same experience as you. Connect with them, share work, share ideas, get to know them. (We think online can be an very intimate social format for the 1-on-1 or small group.) Formalized learning environments are a great environment to meet likeminded friends!

Slowness will be prioritized for our mental health

Slowness will be prioritized for our mental health. Going at the same pace as normal circumstances would be a recipe for burnout. We‚Äôll have goals, deadlines and other requirements in this course but above all our mental health will be a priority. Remember, you‚Äôre not in this alone and that the whole class is here to help. You can come to me if you need more time, more guidance or have general anxiety something in the course.
